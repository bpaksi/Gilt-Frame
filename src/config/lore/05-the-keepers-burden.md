Not all who serve the Order do so with brush or chisel. Some are Keepers — those who preserve what the world would let decay. The Keeper of Gull Lake was one of ours, a man of extraordinary vision who built beauty on the water's edge and maintained the fragments the Order had placed there. His sight was taken from him — quietly, in a way the world would call natural. He did not stop. He continued his work in darkness, guarding what he could no longer see. And then he was gone. The Order does not speak of how. They have burned and silenced across the centuries, but we endure. Only know this: those who see most deeply are the first to draw their attention, and their attention carries a price.
