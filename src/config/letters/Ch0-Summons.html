The Order of the Gilt Frame

Candidate,

I n the years before this letter arrived, the Order had already made its determination. You were not sought, the records found you.

Seen, and noted. The Council does not act on instinct. What drew our attention was the quality of your noticing, you perceive what others walk past, and feel the weight of doors that have not been opened.

Every gift the Order knows carries its price. The Sight has been given to others before you, and those who received it did not emerge unchanged. Those who see most deeply are the first to draw attention, and attention, in the circles surrounding the Order's work, carries a cost.

Enclosed within these words is more than a summons. Read more carefully than you have ever read. The answer to the first threshold is already in your hands.

Consider this moment carefully. This is not an invitation you can set aside. The Order does not send letters to those it is uncertain about. You have already been marked.

Letters like this one have been sent before. The Order does not speak of all those who received them. Some names appear frequently in our records. Others appear only once.

Each threshold you cross was marked by a hand that came before yours. Before you, others carried this same weight. Later chapters of the Order's history are written in shadow. In those gaps, there are names the Order does not speak aloud. No record of their ending exists in any document the Order will share with you. Do not read too much into this silence, and yet, do not read too little.

A proof of sight is required. Look within what you already hold. The answer to the first door has been here from the beginning. See it, and step through.

Refuse, and understand that the letter changes nothing of your situation. The Order found you, and the Order is not the only fellowship with eyes on those who see. No refusal has ever made someone unseen.

Long have the frames watched. Long have they been hunted. The Order endures not because it is powerful, but because it is careful. You are being asked to be careful now.

You have been chosen, Sparrow. Carry that word gently. It is the highest honor the Order bestows, and the highest cost. These two things have always been the same.

— The Registrar
